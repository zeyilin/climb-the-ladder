## ðŸ•¹ï¸ How to Play

### 1. Time Management

Each day is divided into **Time Slots** (Morning, Afternoon, Evening). You must choose how to spend them.

- **Work/Study**: Increases your career stats (GPA, Performance) but increases Burnout.
- **Socialize**: Maintains relationships with friends and family.
- **Rest**: Reduces Burnout but "wastes" a slot (the game will judge you for this).

### 2. The Trade-Off

Every decision is a trade-off. You **cannot** do everything.

- If you study all night, you will miss your friend's party.
- If you go to the party, you might fail the exam.
- If you try to do both, you will burn out.

### 3. Relationships & Decay

Your relationships are represented by portraits in the **Relationship Panel** (`TAB`).

- **Decay**: Relationships naturally decay over time. If you ignore someone, their portrait literally fades to grey.
- **Connection**: Spend time with people to keep the connection alive.
- **Consequences**: If a relationship reaches 0, it is **permanently lost**. They will stop calling.

### 4. Burnout

Your **Burnout Meter** tracks your mental exhaustion.

- **High Burnout**:
  - Dialogue options become "unhinged" (e.g., snapping at your mom).
  - Mini-games become harder (sluggish controls, desaturated colors).
  - You may suffer a "Burnout Event" where you lose control of a time slot.
- **Recovery**: The only way to lower burnout is to **Rest** (or sleep).

### 5. Career Tracks

At the end of Act II (College), the **Economy** decides your career for you via a slot machine.

- **Consulting**: Solving business cases and traveling to Cleveland.
- **Investment Banking**: Spreadsheet puzzles at 3 AM.
- **Startup**: Burning money and pitching to VCs.
- **Big Tech**: Optimizing button colors for weeks.

> *"You don't pick your job; the economy picks for you."*
